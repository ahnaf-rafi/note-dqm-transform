\section{Joint, marginals and conditionals}
\label{sec--joint-marg-cond}

Here, we consider a setting where the observations can be partitioned as \(X =
(Y, Z)\) so that \(\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{Y} \times \mathbf{Z}\) and \(\mathcal{B}
= \mathcal{B}_{\mathbf{Y}} \otimes \mathcal{B}_{\mathbf{Z}}\).
